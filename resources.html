Mental Health Resources

Welcome to our Mental Health Resources page. Here, you’ll find a collection of support services, informative guides, self-help tools, and hotlines aimed at promoting mental well-being.

1. Hotlines and Immediate Help

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-TALK (8255) – Available 24/7 for anyone in crisis.
  • Crisis Text Line: Text "HELLO" to 741741 – Connect with a crisis counselor via text.
  • SAMHSA’s National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357) – Free and confidential treatment referral.

2. Online Counseling Services

  • BetterHelp: Online therapy platform with licensed counselors.
  • Talkspace: Text and video therapy services for various mental health needs.
  • 7 Cups of Tea: Free, anonymous emotional support chatrooms and forums.

3. Informational Resources

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI): Offers educational resources, support groups, and advocacy.
  • Mental Health America (MHA): Resources on understanding mental health conditions, self-help tools, and screenings.
  • Mayo Clinic – Mental Health: Information on mental health conditions and treatment options.

4. Self-Help Tools

  • Moodfit: App for tracking mood, managing stress, and setting goals.
  • Headspace: Meditation and mindfulness exercises to help manage stress and anxiety.
  • Calm: Tools for sleep, relaxation, and anxiety relief.

5. Mental Health Blogs and Podcasts

  • The Mental Illness Happy Hour (Podcast): Stories from people dealing with mental health struggles.
  • Mindful.org (Blog): Articles and resources on practicing mindfulness.
  • Psychology Today (Blog): Professional articles on mental health topics, coping strategies, and expert advice.

6. Books on Mental Health

  • "The Body Keeps the Score" by Bessel van der Kolk: Insights into trauma and healing.
  • "Feeling Good: The New Mood Therapy" by Dr. David D. Burns: Cognitive-behavioral techniques for overcoming depression.
  • "Atomic Habits" by James Clear: Techniques for building healthy habits and improving mental well-being.

7. Self-Assessment Tools

  • Mental Health America’s Screening Tool: Free assessments for anxiety, depression, PTSD, and more.
  • Psychology Today's Self-Tests: Free quizzes on mental health conditions, including stress and relationships.
  • Moodpath: A mobile app that helps track emotions and provides insights on mental health.

8. Articles on Self-Care

  • Self-Care Basics: Practical self-care ideas to support emotional and mental health.
  • Building a Support System: Guide on creating a network of friends, family, and professionals.
  • Mindfulness & Meditation: Techniques for managing stress, including guided meditation exercises.
